# what are conversions? 
Conversions are website goals or objectives. 
Website purpose: 
- Selling products
- Generating leads
- Branding
- Selling advertising
- Building good relationship
- Providing customer support

## 1. Transactional Conversions

Transactional conversions are actions that directly generate revenue or business value. These conversions typically represent the final goal of a marketing campaign.

Examples:

Product Purchase
Insurance Policy Purchase
Subscription Purchase
Loan Approval

Business Impact: Direct revenue generation.

## 2. Non-Transactional Conversions

Non-transactional conversions are actions that do not immediately generate revenue but indicate customer interest or engagement.

Examples:

Newsletter Signup
Contact Form Submission
Account Registration
Brochure Download

Business Impact: Lead generation and customer engagement.

---

Classification Based on Business Goals
## 1. Macro Conversions

Macro conversions represent the primary business objectives that organizations want users to achieve. They are usually the final actions in the customer journey.

Examples:

Product Purchase
Paid Subscription
Insurance Policy Purchase
Loan Application Approval

Macro conversions are often used to measure campaign success and return on investment (ROI).

## 2. Micro Conversions

Micro conversions are smaller actions that indicate progress toward a macro conversion. They help marketers understand user intent and engagement before the final conversion occurs.

Examples:

Product View
Add to Cart
Newsletter Signup
Video View
Downloading a Resource

Micro conversions act as indicators of customer interest and purchase intent.

---

## Direct and Assisted Conversions
## Direct Macro Conversions

A direct macro conversion occurs when a marketing channel directly leads to the final business objective without requiring significant assistance from other channels.

Example:

Google Search Ad → Purchase

In this case, the Google Search Ad receives direct credit for the macro conversion.

## Assisted Macro Conversions

An assisted macro conversion occurs when a channel contributes to the customer journey but is not the final touchpoint before conversion.

Example:

Facebook Ad → Website Visit → Email Campaign → Purchase

Here, Facebook assists the conversion, while Email receives the final conversion credit.

## Direct Micro Conversions

A direct micro conversion occurs when a marketing channel directly causes a micro-level action.

Example:

LinkedIn Ad → Newsletter Signup

The LinkedIn Ad receives direct credit for the micro conversion.

## Assisted Micro Conversions

An assisted micro conversion occurs when a marketing channel helps influence a user who later completes a micro conversion through another channel.

Example:

Display Ad → Website Visit → Organic Search → Newsletter Signup

The Display Ad assists the conversion, while Organic Search receives the final credit.

## Adwords:  

AdWords was the former name of Google's online advertising platform. In 2018, Google renamed it to Google Ads.

It allows businesses to display ads across Google's ecosystem, including:  

Google Search results  
YouTube  
Gmail  
Websites and apps that are part of the Google Display Network  
Google Shopping (for eligible products)  
How it works  

Google Ads uses an auction system. When someone searches for a keyword (for example, "best running shoes"), advertisers who are targeting that keyword compete to show their ads.

Google determines which ads appear based on factors such as:  

Bid: How much the advertiser is willing to pay.  
Ad Quality (Quality Score): How relevant and useful the ad and landing page are.  
Expected impact of ad assets: Such as sitelinks, call buttons, and other extensions.  
Common pricing models  
Cost Per Click (CPC): Pay only when someone clicks your ad.  
Cost Per Mille (CPM): Pay per 1,000 ad impressions.  
Cost Per Acquisition (CPA): Pay based on conversions, often using automated bidding.   
Cost Per View (CPV): Commonly used for video ads on YouTube.  

Example  

Suppose you own a bakery in Hyderabad.  

You create an ad targeting the keyword "birthday cakes near me."   
When someone searches for that phrase, your ad may appear at the top of the search results.  
If they click your ad, you pay Google the amount determined by the auction.  

Benefits  
Reach people who are actively searching for your products or services.  
Set your own daily or monthly budget.  
Target audiences by location, language, interests, device, and more.  
Measure results such as clicks, leads, sales, and return on investment.  

In short, AdWords is simply the old name for Google Ads, Google's platform for creating and managing online advertisements.
